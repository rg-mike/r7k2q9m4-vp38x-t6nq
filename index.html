The core recommendation

Make Gorman Jones discoverable before the owner raises their hand.

The research suggests the competitor set is commercially serious but SEO-light. Many competitors rely on brand searches, principals' names, transaction pages, reports, or press visibility. That leaves room for a more useful owner-facing website that answers the questions founders actually search.

1

Expand the website to win organic search traffic and close more deals.

Build a focused content architecture around valuation, succession, sale process, RIA M&A, buyer types, private equity, roll-ups, aggregators, and books of business.

Why it is worth pursuing

  • Search volume is small but valuable because one qualified owner conversation can be worth more than thousands of generic visitors.
  • Competitors are not consistently owning evergreen educational pages for the highest-fit topics.
  • A deeper site gives paid ads, email, and outreach somewhere credible to send prospects.
  • Content can pre-sell the firm by explaining judgment, process, discretion, and market knowledge before the first call.
2

Nurture existing leads with a monthly or bi-monthly email newsletter.

Turn the website and relationship network into a steady trust-building channel for owners who are interested but not ready.

Why it is worth pursuing

  • RIA M&A decisions are long-cycle, high-trust decisions. A newsletter keeps Gorman Jones present during the quiet evaluation period.
  • Monthly or bi-monthly is enough cadence to stay visible without feeling like a marketing treadmill.
  • Each edition can repurpose one website insight: valuation, buyer behavior, deal terms, succession, or private equity dynamics.
  • The list becomes an owned audience that is not dependent on Google rankings or ad costs.
3

Test 2-3 Google Ads campaigns at about $1,200 per month.

Use paid search as a controlled learning system, not as the primary growth engine. The goal is to find which messages and pages earn qualified conversations.

Why it is worth pursuing

  • Ads can validate search intent months before SEO has enough ranking data.
  • Several keywords show strong commercial bid signals despite modest volume.
  • Exact match keywords will keep spend away from broad consumer financial-advisor traffic.
  • Conversion data can shape the organic content roadmap and newsletter topics.
4

Test targeted cold outreach.

Build narrow lists of high-fit RIA and wealth management firms, then reach owners with useful, non-generic points of view.

Why it is worth pursuing

  • The addressable market is not a secret. The right firms, owners, executives, and trigger events can be identified.
  • Organic search captures active demand; cold outreach can create conversations with firms that are not yet searching.
  • Thoughtful outbound lets Gorman Jones lead with expertise rather than a generic "checking in" pitch.
  • Outbound results will also reveal which value propositions resonate with actual owners.

Targeted cold outreach

The market is small enough to name the accounts.

Cold outreach is worth testing because this is not a faceless mass market. The universe of high-fit RIA and wealth management owners can be segmented by size, founder profile, geography, growth path, recent press, ADV data, and visible succession or capital needs.

Collapse details Expand details

Who to target

Founder-led RIAs, firms with meaningful AUM but limited next-gen visibility, firms showing growth pressure, firms receiving unsolicited buyer interest, and firms that may be debating PE, aggregator, or strategic buyer paths.

What to send

Not a generic pitch. Send a useful point of view: valuation readiness, buyer landscape, succession path, deal structure tradeoffs, or a short founder-specific note tied to a public signal.

How to measure

Track reply quality, booked conversations, newsletter opt-ins, resource clicks, and objections. In a market this small, a few excellent replies matter more than a large open rate.

Outbound should feel like the beginning of a strategic conversation.

The best cold outreach will not say, "Do you want to sell?" It will say, in effect: "We understand the decision you may eventually face, and here is one useful way to think about it before the market forces the question."

Recommended sequence

Build the foundation, then let each channel teach the others.

The channels should not operate as separate marketing experiments. Organic content creates the intellectual property. Ads test search intent. Newsletter nurtures the long-cycle audience. Outbound puts the best ideas in front of named accounts.
